Lasagna

Lasagna is the ultimate comfort food, layers of pasta, rich meat sauce, creamy cheese, and a golden top fresh from the oven. It’s the kind of dish that fills the whole kitchen with a mouth watering aroma and brings everyone running to the table.

Credit: PoiseWinsTitles, May 2011

How To Make Lasagna

You can follow along this simple recipe to make a delicious Lasagna at home

Ingridients

  • Meat: A hearty mix of sweet Italian sausage and lean ground beef gives this lasagna its rich, meaty flavor.
  • Onion and garlic: Chopped onion and a couple of garlic cloves are sautéed with the meat to add depth and aroma.
  • Tomato base: The sauce comes together with crushed tomatoes, tomato sauce, and tomato paste for a full-bodied taste.
  • Sugar: A touch of white sugar balances the acidity of the tomatoes and enhances their natural sweetness.
  • Herbs and spices: Fresh parsley, dried basil, salt, Italian seasoning, fennel seeds, and black pepper create a well-rounded, savory flavor profile.
  • Lasagna noodles: Use your favorite lasagna noodles, whether store-bought or homemade.
  • Cheese: A generous blend of Parmesan, mozzarella, and ricotta makes the dish extra rich and creamy.
  • Egg: Mixing an egg into the ricotta helps hold the cheese layer together when sliced.

How to Make Lasagna Step-By-Step

  1. Preheat & Prep
    • Heat oven to 190°C / 375°F.
    • Lightly grease a 9×13-inch (23×33 cm) baking dish.
    • Gather cheeses: about 425 g (15 oz) ricotta, 450 g (16 oz) mozzarella (sliced or shredded), ¾ cup (75 g) Parmesan. Beat 1 egg into the ricotta with ½ cup Parmesan, 2 Tbsp parsley, a pinch of salt & pepper; set aside.
  2. Make the meat sauce
    • In a large pot over medium-high, brown ~1 kg total meat (half sweet Italian sausage, half lean beef) for 6–8 min, breaking it up. Spoon off excess fat, leaving about 1 Tbsp.
    • Add 1 chopped onion + ½ tsp salt; cook 5–7 min until translucent.
    • Stir in 2–3 minced garlic cloves for 30–45 sec (fragrant).
    • Add crushed tomatoes (28 oz/800 g), tomato sauce (15 oz/425 g), 2 Tbsp tomato paste, 1–2 tsp sugar, 2 tsp dried basil, 1 tsp Italian seasoning, ½ tsp crushed fennel seeds, ¼–½ tsp black pepper, and ½ cup water.
    • Bring to a simmer, then reduce to low and simmer 20–30 min, partially covered, stirring occasionally. The sauce should be thick but spoonable. Taste and adjust salt/sugar.
  3. Cook the noodles (if not using no-boil)
    • Boil 12–14 lasagna noodles in well-salted water until very al dente (about 1–2 min shy of package time; usually 6–7 min).
    • Drain, rinse briefly under cool water, and lay flat on a lightly oiled tray so they don’t stick. Pat dry before layering.
    • Using no-boil noodles? Skip boiling, stir an extra ½ cup water into your sauce, and plan to bake 10 min longer covered.
  4. Set up your cheeses
    • Ricotta layer: the ricotta mixture you made in step 1. If it feels stiff, loosen with 2–3 Tbsp milk.
    • Mozzarella: divide into three portions (for two inner layers + top).
    • Parmesan: reserve about ½ cup for sprinkling between layers and on top.
  5. Assemble the layers
    • Base: Spread 1½ cups meat sauce in the dish.
    • Layer 1: 6 noodles (slightly overlapping) → ½ of the ricotta mixture (spread gently to edges) → ⅓ of the mozzarella → 1½ cups sauce → ¼ cup Parmesan.
    • Layer 2: Repeat the same: noodles → remaining ½ ricotta → ⅓ mozzarella → 1½ cups sauce → ¼ cup Parmesan.
    • Top: Final layer of noodles → a thin blanket of sauce (use what’s left) → remaining mozzarella + Parmesan. Leave a small ½ cm border bare around the edges to reduce boil-over.
  6. Bake
    • Cover the dish tented with foil (lightly oil/spray the foil underside so it won’t stick to the cheese).
    • Bake 25 minutes covered, then remove foil and bake 20–25 minutes more until bubbling around the edges and the top is lightly golden. (Optional: broil 1–2 min at the end for extra color—watch closely.)
  7. Rest & serve
    • Let the lasagna rest 15–20 minutes before cutting—this sets the layers so slices hold together.
    • Garnish with fresh parsley and serve.
